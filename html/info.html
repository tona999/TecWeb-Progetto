Calculator Instructions

In this page we will describe how our calculator works. To do so, we are going to save a recipe for an Apple Pie, step by step. Then, we will calculate the insulin dose needed for a certain quantity of Apple Pie, and vice versa.

Please note that the calculations are approximated. Use with caution.

Here is the list of ingredients we are going to use:

Sample list of ingredients
Ingredient Quantity
Apples (peeled, cored and sliced) 440g
Brown Sugar 50g
All-purpose Flour 1/4 cup (32g)
Ground Nutmeg 20g
store-bought Pie Crusts 2
Unsalted Butter 1 tablespoon (15g)
Eggs 1
Milk 1 tablespoon

Ingredients

First of all, head over to Your Ingredients section and save the new ingredients by filling in the necessary information and clicking the Save New Ingredient button.

For each ingredient insert its name and the quantity of carbohydrates (carbs for short) in 100g of product. You can find this information in the nutritional table on the product’s package. The value we are interested in is the quantity of carbs, and not the quantity of sugars. Here is an example of a nutritional table you can find on a bag of cookies:

Nutrition Facts per 100gr of product
Component Quantity
Protein 5g
Carbohydrates 72g
  of which Sugars 37.5g
Fat 20g
  of which Saturated Fatty Acids 10g
Cholesterol 0mg

If you fail finding this information, or if the product does not come in a package, try looking it up on Calorie King or simply google it. For example, all-purpose flour contains approximately 73g of carbs in 100g of flour, so saving the new ingredient will look like this: On the other hand, unsalted butter has less than 1g of carbs in 100g of product. In this case butter is irrelevant for our calculations but we are going to insert it anyway for the sake of completeness. Your Ingredients section should now look like this: After you are done saving the ingredients we can proceed making the recipe. To do so, open the Calculator either by clicking the Calculator button or by clicking the Make a New Recipe button in Your Recipes section.

Calculator

Initially the recipe in the Calculator is empty and we need to add the ingredients.

In the Calculator window you can see a dropdown list. Click on the list and pick the ingredients you just created, one by one. Notice that a new card appears for each ingredient, like so:

Fields

Each card is composed of four sections. The first section contains the name of the ingredient.

The Nutrition Facts section contains the quantity of carbs in 100g of product, which is the information you inserted before. These two sections are already filled out when you insert the ingredient from the dropdown list. The Grammage section is the most important one. In the Grams field insert the quantity of ingredient needed for your recipe. For example, we need 440g of apples for our Apple Pie, so we will insert the number 440 in the Grams field. Note that the Insulin Units field, on the right, now contains a value. This value represents the dose of insulin required for 440g of apples. This is perhaps the value you will care about the most. The last section allows you to cut your ingredient into a number of pieces. Press the plus button to increase the number of pieces or directly write in the number in the Pieces field. Note that the table now contains three values:

  • carbs: the quantity of carbs of one piece measured in grams;
  • grams: the weight of one piece measured in grams;
  • units: the insulin units needed for one piece.

This sections is not particularly interesting at the moment, but it will be soon! Note that liquids, for example milk, are measured in milliliters and not in grams, but don't worry, it is the proportion between the Nutritional Facts data that counts, and not the unit of measurement, therefore the calculator can be used for liquids as well!

Recipe

After inserting all the ingredients your calculator should look like this: Now that we have inserted all the ingredients in the Calculator it’s time to combine them together! On the bottom of the page you can see the calculator’s control panel. Find the Combine button and click it. A new ingredient-like card will be shown. This card describes your recipe as a homogeneous combination of ingredients or, simply put, it describes the recipe as a single ingredient/product. We still haven’t saved our recipe though. To do so, insert the name “Grandma’s Apple Pie” in the Recipe Name field of the card and click the Save button. The recipe has now been saved and can be found in Your Recipes section.

Recipe fields

Now let's look closely at the Recipe card that was displayed in the calculator.

In the Nutrition Facts section the Calculator has automatically calculated the nutritional facts of our Apple Pie, based on the ingredients data we inserted before.

The Grammage section is the most important part of the whole Calculator. Here you can calculate the dose of insulin units you need to inject for eating a certain quantity of Apple Pie, and vice versa.

Let’s say you plan on eating 50g of Apple Pie. Insert 50 in the Grammage field and note that the Insulin Units field now contains 1.51. This means that for 50 grams of Apple Pie you need to inject 1.63 units of insulin. 1.63 units is not very useful though, as you can not inject half a unit. In this case, you could for example decide to eat the equivalent of 2 insulin units. No problem, just insert the number of insulin units you plan on injecting in the Insulin Units field. The Grams field will then tell you how much Apple Pie to eat for that particular dose of insulin.

Insert the number 2 in the Insulin Units field and note that the Grammage field contains the number 61. This means that if you inject 2 insulin units you need to eat approximately 60g of Apple Pie. Cool, right? You can also apply the same procedure if you follow a strict diet and the quantity of food you eat depends on a fixed dose of insulin.

Pieces number

The last section allows you to cut the Apple Pie into pieces. Before we do that though, we need to tell the Calculator how much of the Apple Pie we actually have, exactly as we did before with the ingredients. Insert the grammage of your Apple Pie in the Grammage field. If you haven't already eaten any of the Apple Pie then the grammage is equivalent to the sum of the ingredients grammages. This sum has already been automatically calculated in the Nutrition Facts section of the Recipe card. If you haven't eaten any of the Apple Pie yet, insert 782 in the Grammage Field in the Grammage section, otherwise weigh how much of the apple cake you have left and insert that value in the Grammage Field. One way to use the last section is to determine the number of pieces you need to cut your cake into in order to have a certain dose of insulin for each piece. Let’s say you want every piece of pie to require approximately 2 insulin units. Click on the plus sign to increase the number of pieces until every piece requires approximately 2 insulin units. In our case, you would need to cut the Apple Pie into 12 equivalent pieces in order for each piece to require 2 insuilin units approximately. Moreover, each piece should weight approximately 65 grams and has approximately 25 grams of carbs in it. You could also use this section is to calculate the information for every piece given the number of pieces you have already cut your cake into. Please note that for the Calculator to work properly you need to make homogeneous recipes, which means that all the ingredients need to be evenly distributed in the recipe. For example, a piece of pie with an apple on top of it will have more carbs than a piece of pie without an apple, which is not what we want.

This section can be particularly useful if you buy a product that has many ingredients inside of it as well. Imagine you buy a package of cookies and you want to know the information about each cookie. Insert the nutritional facts and the grammage of the the whole package, as we saw before, and then insert the number of cookies in the Pieces field. There you go, you now have the relevant information for each cookie in the table.

You can edit, save and delete your recipes at any time from Your Recipes section.
